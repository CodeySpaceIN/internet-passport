# Internet Passport: Investor Memo (One Page)

## Thesis
Internet Passport is a universal trust layer for an internet increasingly filled with synthetic actors. We verify who or what is behind accounts, content, and actions (human, organization, or AI agent) without forcing full identity disclosure.

As AI-generated content, deepfakes, agent automation, and impersonation rise, platforms face a structural trust deficit. The winner in this category becomes core internet plumbing: authenticity, accountability, and provenance as APIs.

## Problem
The internet's highest-cost failure mode is no longer content scarcity, but trust scarcity:

- Impersonation and account takeover in support, commerce, and social channels
- Deepfake and voice-clone abuse in hiring, payments, and approvals
- AI agents performing sensitive actions without clear identity or policy context
- Weak auditability for high-risk workflows (who did what, under what authorization)

Current defenses are fragmented (CAPTCHA, ad hoc KYC, siloed fraud tools) and not composable across platforms.

## Product
Internet Passport provides trust primitives as APIs and SDKs:

- Verified human and organization signals with selective disclosure
- Verified AI agent identity and signed action records
- Content/session trust verdicts with explainable reason codes
- Provenance and signature workflows for high-risk actions
- Trust dashboard for policy, review, and dispute handling

Positioning: Stripe-for-trust / Cloudflare-for-authenticity.

## Beachhead and GTM
Start with one acute, measurable pain: B2B support and internal AI-agent operations.

Why this wedge:

- Direct fraud and operational risk tied to support and agent actions
- Clear buyers: Security, Trust & Safety, CX Ops, Compliance
- Fast ROI proof: reduced fraud losses, faster escalations, stronger audit readiness

Land with API + dashboard in one workflow; expand to adjacent trust surfaces (onboarding, payments approval, marketplace verification, creator authenticity).

## Business Model

- Usage-based API pricing per verification and risk decision
- SaaS seats for trust operations dashboard
- Enterprise contracts (SSO, policy controls, data residency, compliance packages)
- Premium portability features for user trust wallet (later phase)

Long-term upside: network effects in risk models and trust graph coverage.

## Defensibility

- Data flywheel from cross-surface trust events and adjudications
- Developer lock-in via SDK/workflow integration into critical paths
- Cryptographic action and provenance standards that become ecosystem defaults
- Enterprise distribution through compliance and governance requirements

## 12-18 Month Milestones

- 3-5 design partners in support/AI-agent-heavy environments
- Production-grade Trust Card API and Signed Action Ledger
- SOC 2 and enterprise procurement readiness
- First protocol/SDK integrations (GitHub, Slack, internal tooling)
- Repeatable fraud-loss reduction case studies

## Why Now
AI made synthetic identity/content cheap and scalable. Trust verification has become mandatory infrastructure for every platform where money, decisions, or reputation move.

One-line pitch: We are building the trust infrastructure for an internet full of humans, bots, and AI agents.
